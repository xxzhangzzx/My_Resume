02 / Current Build

ONGOING

LOCAL ORCHESTRATION · GENERATIVE VIDEO · CONTINUITY

Script-to-Video
Pipeline

The goal is an end-to-end workflow that accepts a Word screenplay and reference assets, plans the production, generates each shot, repairs weak transitions, and exports a coherent finished video with minimal manual intervention.

01 working

Parse

Read screenplay text and tables from .docx, identify scenes, characters, actions, locations, and production constraints.

02 working

Plan

Convert scenes into a shot graph with camera framing, duration, motion, continuity groups, reference rules, and stable end states.

03 working

Generate

Create opening frames, edited continuation frames, and video candidates through image and video generation models.

04 current focus

Validate

Score candidates for character, wardrobe, environment, prop state, action completion, framing, and temporal stability.

05 current focus

Bridge

Reuse an approved tail frame or generate a short camera/action bridge when two independently generated clips do not join naturally.

06 next

Finish

Standardize resolution, frame rate, visual tone, handles, subtitles, audio, and final sequencing through FFmpeg.

THE VISION

A production system, not a collection of isolated generations.

Each shot should inherit the world state established by the previous shot. The system maintains a shared character and scene specification, passes approved visual state forward, records generation decisions, and retries only the stage that failed instead of restarting the entire sequence.

A

Cross-shot continuity

Preserve the same character, clothing, architecture, lighting, prop positions, screen direction, and action state across clips generated at different times.

B

Reliable tail frames

Inspect late-frame candidates near 90%, 95%, and 100% of a clip, reject unstable endings, and select the strongest frame as the next shot's visual starting state.

C

Transition repair

When a direct cut exposes a pose, camera, or action mismatch, synthesize a short bridge shot from the previous tail and next opening rather than hiding the failure with an arbitrary edit.

D

Failure-aware automation

Detect duplicate characters, sudden appearance, incomplete actions, deformed frames, visual drift, and prompt-length/API failures before they propagate downstream.
